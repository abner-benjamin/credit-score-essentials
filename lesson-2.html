Lesson 2

The 5 Factors That Make Up Your Score

Your credit score isn’t random — it’s built from five categories that reflect your habits over time. Understanding these helps you take control.

Flip the Cards to Find Out More

Payment History

35%

Whether you’ve paid your bills on time. Missed payments hurt more than you might think. Even one late payment can lower your score significantly.
‍
Example: Paying your credit card 30 days late? That stays on your report for up to 7 years.

Credit Utilization

30%

How much of your available credit you’re using. This shows how responsible you are with your limits.
‍
Example: If you have a $1,000 limit and owe $700, your utilization is 70% — too high. Ideal is under 30%.

Length of Credit History

15%

How long you’ve had credit accounts open. Older is better. This shows stability and experience.
‍
Example: A credit card from college helps you more than a new one you opened last week.

Credit Mix

10%

The variety of credit types you have. This includes cards, loans, auto financing, etc.
‍
Example: Having both a credit card and a student loan is better than just one or the other.

New Credit

10%

How often you’ve applied for credit recently. Every application creates a "hard inquiry," which can lower your score temporarily.
‍
Example: Applying for five cards in one month? That looks risky to lenders.

Combined, all of these factors are used to calculate your credit score by the three credit bureaus: TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax
